Life of Paulo Coelho

  • 1947: Born on August 24th in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.
  • 1960s-1970s: Explores various paths including theater, music, and writing.
  • 1982: Publishes "The Pilgrimage," his first book, detailing his transformative journey along the Camino de Santiago.
  • 1988: "The Alchemist" is released, becoming a global phenomenon, translated into numerous languages, and selling millions of copies worldwide.
  • 1990s: Continues to publish impactful works like "Brida," "The Valkyries," and "By the River Piedra I Sat Down and Wept."
  • 2003: Receives the Crystal Award from the World Economic Forum for his outstanding contribution to the arts.
  • 2010s: Publishes "Aleph," "Manuscript Found in Accra," and "Adultery," exploring themes of spirituality, love, and personal fulfillment.
  • 2016: Named one of the most influential people in the world by TIME magazine.
  • 2020: Despite his passing on August 24th, his words continue to inspire millions, leaving an indelible mark on literature and hearts worldwide.
